# Service Offerings

Three productized services at fixed scope and price. Predictable for clients. Repeatable for you.

## 1 · Design System Audit

Who it's for: Teams with an existing component library that feels inconsistent, hard to maintain, or has grown without a clear architecture.
What they get: A structured written report covering component consistency, accessibility gaps (WCAG 2.1 AA), token structure, documentation quality, and a prioritized remediation roadmap.

Price: $1,500 flat
Timeline: 5 business days
Format: PDF report + 60-min debrief call

### Why clients pay for this:

- Accessibility violations carry legal risk — WCAG compliance is increasingly mandated
- Engineering teams waste hours on inconsistency they can't easily quantify
- The audit pays for itself if it prevents one accessibility lawsuit or one sprint of rework

## 2 · Component Library Starter

Who it's for: Startups or small teams starting from scratch, or teams whose existing library is beyond repair and needs a clean foundation.
What they get: 20 core components built to their design tokens, accessible out of the box (WCAG 2.1 AA), documented with usage guidelines, and ready to extend.

Price: $3,500 flat
Timeline: 2–3 weeks
Stack: React + your chosen styling solution

Includes:
Design token architecture (color, spacing, typography, radius)
20 components: buttons, inputs, modals, cards, navigation, alerts, and more
Accessibility attributes and keyboard navigation baked in
Storybook documentation with usage examples
One round of revisions

## 3 · Design-to-Code Workflow Optimization

Who it's for: Teams where the Figma-to-code handoff is a constant source of friction — mismatched specs, missing tokens, designer/developer miscommunication.
What they get: An audit of their current handoff process, Figma file structure review, Code Connect setup (if applicable), and a written workflow guide their team can follow.

Price: $2,000 flat
Timeline: 1 week
Format: Process audit + workflow guide + implementation session

### Why this sells:

Every product team using Figma has this problem
The cost of friction is invisible until someone quantifies it
You have direct, hands-on experience solving exactly this
